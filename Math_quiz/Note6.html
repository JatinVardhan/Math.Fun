Question 1:

What is the value of π (pi) to two decimal places?

Answer: π (pi) is approximately 3.14.

Question 2:

If a triangle has angles of 30°, 60°, and 90°, what type of triangle is it?

Answer: The triangle is a right-angled triangle. The angles 30°, 60°, and 90° are characteristic of a right triangle.

Question 3:

What is the value of the square root of 121?

Answer: The square root of 121 is 11, as 11 multiplied by itself equals 121.

Question 4:

In an arithmetic progression, if a = 3 and d = 4, what is the seventh term?

Answer: In an arithmetic progression, the nth term (aₙ) can be calculated using the formula aₙ = a + (n − 1) d. Substituting a = 3, d = 4, and n = 7, we get aₙ = 3 + (7 − 1) × 4 = 3 + 24 = 27.

Question 5:

What is the result of 25(2 raised to the power of 5)?

Answer: 25 is equal to 32. This is obtained by multiplying 2 by itself five times.

Question 6:

If log2(x)=3, what is the value of x?

Answer:log2 (x) = 3, means that 23 = x. Therefore, x = 8.

Question 7:

What is the area of a rectangle with length 8 units and width 5 units?

Answer: The area (A) of a rectangle is calculated as A= length X width. In this case, A= 8 X 5 = 40 square units.

Question 8:

If sin(45°)=cos(45°), what is the value of tan(45°)?

Answer: tan(45°) is equal to 1. This is derived from the trigonometric identity tan(θ) = cos(θ)/sin(θ), and since sin(45°) = cos(45°), tan(45°) = 1/1 = 1.

Question 9:

What is the factorial of 6 (6!)?

Answer: 6! = 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 720.

Question 10:

In a right-angled triangle, if the legs are 5 units and 12 units, what is the length of the hypotenuse?

Answer: The length of the hypotenuse (c) in a right-angled triangle can be found using the Pythagorean theorem: c = √( a2 + b2 ). Substituting a = 5 and b = 12, we get c = √( 52 + 122 ) = √( 25 + 144 ) = √169 = 13.

Question 11:

What is the perimeter of a square with sides of length 8 units?

Answer: The perimeter (P) of a square is calculated as P=4 × side length. In this case, P = 4 × 8 = 32 units.

Question 12:

Calculate the area of a circle with a radius of 5 units. (Use π ≈ 3.14)

Answer: The area (A) of a circle is given by the formula A = πr2. Substituting r = 5 and π ≈ 3.14, we get A ≈ 3.14 × 52 = 3.14 × 25 ≈ 78.5 square units.

Question 13:

Solve for x: 2x + 5 = 13.

Answer:To solve for x, subtract 5 from both sides of the equation: 2x + 5 − 5 = 13 − 5. Simplifying, 2x = 8. Divide both sides by 2: x = 4.

Question 14:

What is the sum of the angles in a triangle?

Answer: The sum of the angles in a triangle is always 180 degrees.

Question 15:

If log₃ (9) = x, what is the value of x?

Answer:log3 (9) = x means that 3x = 9. Therefore, x = 2.

Question 16:

Answer: sin (60°) = √ 3 / 2 or approximately 0.866.

Question 17:

Calculate the factorial of 7 (7!).

Answer:7! = 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 50.

Question 18:

What is the next term in the arithmetic sequence: 5, 9, 13, 17, ...?

Answer: The common difference in this arithmetic sequence is 4. Adding 4 to the last term (17), the next term is 21.

Question 19:

Determine the square root of 144.

Answer:The square root of 144 is 12, as 12 × 12 = 144.

Question 20:

In a right-angled triangle, if one angle is 45 degrees, what is the measure of the other acute angle?
